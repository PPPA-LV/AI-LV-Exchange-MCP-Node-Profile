# Declared interests

This register implements **§16 of the AI LV Exchange MCP Node Profile** and **§2 of [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)**.

Everyone who proposes normative change to this profile declares their interests here. There is no exception for editors, for the chair, or for PPPA.

**Declaring an interest does not disqualify anyone.** It is the mechanism by which a person who is both an author of a standard and a supplier of a product implementing it can participate legitimately. What disqualifies someone is concealing an interest — or voting on a change that advantages their own product.

Anyone may open an issue to challenge an entry, or to point out an interest they believe is missing.

## What must be declared

An interest exists where the contributor, their employer, or an entity they control:

- sells or intends to sell an implementation of this profile;
- sells or intends to sell a product whose adoption this profile would advantage;
- is an Exchange participant, or is applying to become one;
- performs, or expects to perform, Scheme Registrar or conformity assessment functions;
- has any other interest a reasonable reader would want to know about.

## What recusal means

A contributor with a declared interest **participates fully in discussion** — their technical judgement is often exactly what is needed — and **withdraws from the decision** on any change that would:

1. make their implementation mandatory or advantaged;
2. exclude a competing implementation;
3. assess the conformity of their own product;
4. accredit their own service.

Recusal is recorded in the minutes of the decision.

## Appeals

A decision taken in breach of this register may be appealed under CONTRIBUTING §8. The review body **excludes any person with a commercial interest** in the affected node or in a competing implementation.
